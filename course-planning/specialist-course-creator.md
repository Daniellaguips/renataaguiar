# Online Course Creator Specialist — Analysis & Recommendations

## COURSE 1: EFT & TFT Professional Training ($1,370)

### Structure & Pacing

The 5-lesson structure mirrors the original live class format too closely. At roughly 2 hours per lesson, you are asking async students to maintain focus for far too long in a single sitting. Live instruction has built-in engagement (eye contact, real-time Q&A, social pressure to stay seated). Async has none of that.

**Recommendation: Expand to 8 modules, each broken into 15-20 minute video segments.** Here is a suggested restructure:

- **Module 1 — Why This Matters** (Foundations, science, emotional architecture). Pull the body awareness exercise out as a standalone "Start Here" welcome video.
- **Module 2 — The Tapping Map** (All points, demonstration, mirror practice). This deserves its own module because it is the core physical skill. Students need to drill this before anything else.
- **Module 3 — Measuring & Self-Application** (SUD/SUE scales, EFT sequence, setup phrases). Currently split across Lessons 1 and 2 — consolidate.
- **Module 4 — TFT Algorithms & the 9-Gamut** (Currently crammed into Lesson 2 alongside EFT protocols, which is too much new technique in one sitting).
- **Module 5 — The Emotional Cycle & Tuning** (The theoretical backbone of Lesson 3, given room to breathe).
- **Module 6 — Practitioner Skills** (Rapport, listening, questioning — currently sharing space with the emotional cycle content, but this is a distinct skill set).
- **Module 7 — Professional Sessions & Difficult Cases** (Session structure, resistant clients, ethics).
- **Module 8 — Building Your Practice** (Case presentations, business model, next steps, closing).

This solves three problems at once: each module has a single clear learning objective, the total content per sitting drops to 45-60 minutes, and students get more "completion dopamine" with 8 checkpoints instead of 5.

**Pacing concern within lessons:** Lesson 2 is the most overloaded. It tries to cover the full EFT sequence, SUD practice, a 10-step mini-session protocol, TFT algorithms for six conditions, AND the 9-Gamut treatment. That is at least three distinct skill sets. A student watching this async will either skim or abandon. Split it.

### Student Engagement

The current design relies heavily on elements that only work live: "live measurement," "timed pair practice," "live case presentations with correction." Every one of these needs an async equivalent.

**Specific additions needed:**

- **Practice-and-record assignments.** After learning the tapping points in Module 2, students should record themselves doing the full sequence on video and submit it. This replaces "live demonstration and practice." Use a simple upload mechanism (Loom links work fine).
- **SUD/SUE tracking worksheets.** Give students a PDF or fillable form where they log their SUD scores before and after each self-application session across the week. This creates data they can see, which sustains motivation.
- **Case study response prompts.** The Carlos, Ana, Mariana, Roberto, and Fernanda case studies are strong. But in async, students just passively read them. Instead: present the case, then ask the student to write their treatment plan BEFORE revealing the model answer. This is the single highest-impact engagement technique for professional training courses.
- **Peer practice partnerships.** Assign students into pairs (or triads) at enrollment. The "timed pair practice" in Lesson 3 can become a scheduled Zoom call between partners, with a structured worksheet to guide the session. You provide the framework; they provide each other.
- **Module quizzes.** Short (5-7 questions), focused on clinical judgment rather than recall. Example: "A client reports SUD of 8 after three rounds of tapping with no change. What is your next step?" Multiple choice with explanations for each answer.
- **A private community space** (Circle, Slack, or a simple Facebook group). Professional training courses live or die by peer community. Students need a place to post practice reports, ask questions, and feel accountable.

### Multimedia Strategy

For a $1,370 professional certification course, production quality matters, but not in the way most creators think. Students do not need cinematic B-roll. They need clear demonstrations, visible tapping points, and the instructor's face.

**Video types needed:**

1. **Talking head with slides** for conceptual content (science, emotional cycle, ethics). Keep these under 15 minutes each.
2. **Demonstration videos** showing tapping sequences from a camera angle where students can see the hands and face simultaneously. These are the highest-value assets in the course. Invest in good lighting and a close-up angle.
3. **"Practice along" videos** where the instructor does a full sequence at learning pace, pausing for the student to follow. These should be separate from the teaching videos so students can replay them without re-watching the explanation.
4. **Case study walkthrough videos** (screen share or picture-in-picture over a written case). The instructor reads the case, then talks through clinical reasoning aloud. This models expert thinking.

**Downloadable resources:**

- Tapping points reference card (printable, laminated-quality PDF — students will use this in sessions with their own clients)
- Complete EFT sequence checklist
- TFT algorithm quick-reference by condition
- 10-step mini-session protocol as a one-page flowchart
- SUD/SUE tracking log
- Professional session template (the 5-phase structure as a fillable PDF)
- Ethics guidelines document
- Business setup checklist (pricing, niche, program structure)

**Audio:** Provide audio-only versions of the conceptual lessons for on-the-go review. Do NOT provide audio-only for demonstration content — those require visual.

### Completion Rate Optimization

Professional certification courses have a natural advantage: students paid $1,370 and want the credential. But async completion rates still typically hover around 30-40% without intervention.

**Strategies:**

- **Drip content weekly.** Do not unlock all 8 modules at once. Release one per week. This creates a cohort rhythm even in async and prevents the "I'll binge it later" trap that kills completion.
- **Homework gates.** Require submission of the practice assignment before unlocking the next module. This is the single most effective completion driver for professional courses, and it is justified because you are certifying practitioners. You need to verify competence.
- **Weekly email sequence.** One email per module week: recap of key concepts, encouragement, reminder of the assignment, and a teaser for next week. Keep these short (150-200 words).
- **Mid-course check-in.** Between Modules 4 and 5, schedule an optional live Q&A call (or office hours). This is where wavering students get re-engaged. Record it for those who cannot attend live.
- **Certification as the finish line.** Make the certificate contingent on completing all assignments, not just watching all videos. This gives the final modules real stakes.

### The Async Challenge — Specific Adaptations

The biggest risk in converting this course is Lesson 5's "live case presentations from students with correction." This is where the real learning happens in a live format, and it has no easy async equivalent.

**Solution: A capstone video submission.** Students record a 15-minute mock session (with a volunteer or practice partner) and submit it. The instructor (or a trained reviewer) provides written or video feedback. This is labor-intensive but essential for a course at this price point. If scaling becomes an issue, create a detailed rubric and train senior graduates to do peer review.

## COURSE 2: Rebirth of the Soul ($1,290)

### Structure & Pacing

The consolidation from 20 chapters to 6 lessons is the right instinct, but the current grouping has problems.

**Lesson 2 (Chapters 4-7) is doing too much.** It covers self-love, narrative building, limiting beliefs, AND reconnecting with dreams. These are four distinct transformation processes. A student cannot meaningfully do the inner work on all four in a single week.

**Lesson 6 (Chapters 17-20) is the weakest grouping.** It lumps "self-care strengthens health," "self-esteem in practice," "healthy relationships," and "final message" together. The relationships chapter belongs with the other relationships content (Chapters 10, 13, 14), and the "in practice" chapter should be the actual capstone, not buried alongside a health chapter.

**Recommended restructure — 8 lessons:**

- **Lesson 1 (Ch 1-2): The Truth About Self-Esteem** — What it is, when and why it breaks. This creates the "diagnosis" that motivates everything after.
- **Lesson 2 (Ch 3-4): Rebuilding From the Ground Up** — Conscious care and self-love as the foundation. Two tightly related chapters.
- **Lesson 3 (Ch 5-6): Rewriting Your Story** — Narrative and limiting beliefs. These are two sides of the same process.
- **Lesson 4 (Ch 7-8): Dreams & Confidence** — Reconnecting with aspirations and building the confidence to pursue them. Natural pairing.
- **Lesson 5 (Ch 9, 15, 17): The Practice of Self-Care** — Daily habits, self-praise, and health. Currently scattered across the book but thematically unified.
- **Lesson 6 (Ch 10, 13, 14, 19): Relationships & Boundaries** — All relationship content in one place: their importance, how to build them, setting limits, and maintaining health.
- **Lesson 7 (Ch 11-12): Breaking Free** — Comparison and fear. These are the two biggest saboteurs, and they deserve focused attention without competing topics.
- **Lesson 8 (Ch 16, 18, 20): Your Rebirth** — Rebuilding, practice integration, and the closing message. This becomes a true capstone.

### Student Engagement

This course has a critical challenge: it is a personal development course for women at $1,290. At this price, students expect transformation, not just information.

**What is missing:**

- **Guided journaling prompts with structure.** Each lesson should have 2-3 specific journaling prompts with sentence starters.
- **Audio-guided exercises.** The self-reflection and letter-writing exercises should be available as guided audio tracks (5-10 minutes each).
- **A private women's community.** Non-negotiable at this price point.
- **Weekly "reflection share" prompts** in the community.
- **Progress rituals.** At the end of each lesson, a short ritual or symbolic action.
- **Self-assessment at start and end.** A simple 10-question self-esteem inventory taken in Lesson 1 and again in Lesson 8.

### Multimedia Strategy

**Video types:**

1. **Direct-to-camera "conversation" videos.** Warm lighting, close framing, no desk or podium. 10-15 minutes maximum.
2. **Guided exercise videos.** The instructor walks through a journaling or reflection exercise in real time, with pauses. 5-8 minutes.
3. **Story/testimony videos.** Short (3-5 minute) videos with relevant personal or client transformation stories.
4. **Daily affirmation videos.** 1-2 minute videos, one per lesson. Also excellent for social media clips.

**Downloadable resources:**

- Guided journaling workbook (beautifully designed PDF)
- Daily affirmation cards (printable, one set per lesson)
- Self-esteem self-assessment
- "My New Narrative" worksheet
- Boundary-setting scripts and templates
- Self-care habit tracker
- Relationship health checklist

## Cross-Course Synergy

1. **Reframe the gift as a gateway, not a bonus.** Position it as: "Your certification includes a complete self-esteem protocol you can use with your own clients."
2. **Create a "client edition" of the manual.** A version that EFT practitioners can give to their own clients.
3. **Cross-promote in the other direction.** Students in the Rebirth course should receive an invitation to the EFT course at a discount.
4. **Shared community, separate spaces.**
5. **Bundle pricing.** Both courses together at a discount.
