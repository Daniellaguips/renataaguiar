# EFT/TFT Clinical Instructor — Analysis & Recommendations

## Overall Assessment

This is a well-structured introductory course with a solid conceptual arc: foundations, self-application, practitioner stance, professional sessions, and integration. The progression is logical, the language is accessible, and the course clearly comes from someone who understands both the techniques and how adults learn. However, as a certification-level professional training priced at $1,370 and marketed to therapists becoming practitioners, there are significant gaps in clinical depth, supervised practice, safety infrastructure, and alignment with recognized credentialing bodies.

---

## 1. Clinical Completeness

**Strengths:**
- The full tapping point map with meridian associations is included.
- The 10-step mini-session protocol and 5-phase professional session structure give students a clear procedural framework.
- The SUDs/SUE dual measurement is good practice; many courses only teach SUDs.
- The inclusion of both EFT and TFT in one course offers breadth that most trainings lack.

**Critical Gaps:**

- **No coverage of the EFT "Tell the Story" technique.** Core gentle technique in Clinical EFT for working with traumatic memory without flooding.
- **No "Movie Technique."** Foundational trauma protocol in EFT. Both techniques are designed to titrate exposure and are essential for safe trauma work.
- **No Tearless Trauma technique.** The third leg of the gentle trauma triad in Clinical EFT.
- **No coverage of aspects.** The concept that a single issue has multiple sensory, emotional, and cognitive aspects — each may need separate tapping.
- **No discussion of the "Generalization Effect."** Once you collapse enough specific events, the general pattern shifts.
- **No "Personal Peace Procedure."** Systematic approach (listing and tapping through all bothersome memories).
- **Collarbone Breathing Procedure is absent.** Standard TFT correction procedure for treatment resistance.
- **No discussion of energy toxins.** Part of the TFT canon and should be presented and critically discussed.
- **Floor-to-ceiling eye roll** (TFT closing/consolidation technique) is not mentioned.
- **No coverage of the "Choices Method"** (Dr. Patricia Carrington).

**Recommendation:** Add a dedicated module covering the Movie Technique, Tell the Story, Tearless Trauma, aspects, the Generalization Effect, and the Personal Peace Procedure. These are core Level 1/Level 2 techniques in every recognized EFT training.

---

## 2. Safety Protocols

**Strengths:**
- Caution note on the trauma TFT algorithm.
- Ethics mention of suicide ideation referral and scope of practice.
- Mariana case study models appropriate containment.

**Critical Gaps:**

- **No abreaction protocol.** No systematic protocol for managing intense emotional reactions: how to ground, when to stop tapping, when to shift to breathing or bilateral tapping only, "squeeze and breathe" technique, how to close safely after abreaction.

- **No contraindications list.** Should explicitly address: active psychosis, dissociative disorders (especially DID), severe personality disorders, active substance intoxication, recent cardiac events, seizure disorders, and pregnancy.

- **No dissociation screening or management.** Must be able to recognize dissociation (glazed eyes, flat affect, sudden calm that is not resolution, loss of time orientation) and respond appropriately.

- **No informed consent template or discussion.** Professional practice requires written informed consent covering: nature of EFT/TFT, evidence base and limitations, not a substitute for medical/psychological treatment, practitioner's qualifications, client's right to stop.

- **No grounding/stabilization techniques.** Need: 5-4-3-2-1 sensory grounding, bilateral tapping without verbal content, orienting to the room, breathing techniques beyond the 4-2-6 mentioned.

- **No session safety planning.** What happens if a client discloses abuse, self-harm, or harm to others mid-session? Mandatory reporting obligations, holding the therapeutic frame while initiating referral, ending a session safely.

- **No guidance on remote/online sessions.** Ensuring private space, emergency contact on file, knowing client's physical location, managing technology failures.

**Recommendation:** Create a dedicated safety module (before Lesson 4) covering abreaction management, contraindications, dissociation recognition, grounding techniques, informed consent, and session safety planning. This is non-negotiable.

---

## 3. Algorithm Accuracy

**Specific Issues:**

- **Anxiety algorithm (EB, UE, CB + 9-Gamut) is simplified.** In Callahan's published work, typically: under-eye (UE), under-arm (UA), collarbone (CB) + 9-Gamut + repeat UE-UA-CB. The omission of the under-arm point is a substantive error.

- **Depression algorithm (Gamut x30, CB x5 + 9-Gamut)** — tapping the Gamut point 30 times is unusual. In Callahan's standard, the sequence typically begins with under-eye or specific meridian points, not extended Gamut tapping. Needs citation or correction.

- **Anger algorithm (Pinky x5, CB x5 + 9-Gamut)** is recognizable as simplified, though may also include the under-eye point.

- **Physical pain algorithm (Gamut x50, CB x5 + 9-Gamut)** — Gamut x50 is not standard in published TFT sources. Needs verification.

- **9-Gamut sequence is correct** in components.

- **Missing: SUD check between sequences.** In TFT, measure SUD after each treatment sequence and before deciding next steps.

- **Missing: concept of "perturbation"** in Callahan's theoretical framework.

**Recommendation:** Cross-reference all TFT algorithms against Callahan's published texts. Either correct to match published sources or explicitly label as "adapted from TFT." Add SUD checkpoints within algorithm instructions.

## 4. Practice Hours

**This is the most significant structural weakness.**

- 5 weekly lessons with asynchronous delivery provides essentially zero supervised practice hours.
- ACEP requires documented practice hours with real clients, supervision, and case submissions.
- EFTi Level 1 and Level 2 each require multiple days of in-person training. Level 2 requires minimum 50 mentoring hours.
- AAMET requires 12+ hours Level 1, 18+ hours Level 2, plus mentoring and assessment.
- This course provides approximately 5-10 hours of content with perhaps 30 minutes of structured practice.

**What should be added:**
- Minimum 20 practice sessions documented in a practice log
- At least 3-5 supervised sessions with feedback
- A case study portfolio (3-5 written case studies)
- A skills assessment or demonstration video
- Ongoing peer practice groups

**Recommendation:** Either significantly expand practice or reframe as "foundational training" rather than "professional training."

## 6. Case Study Quality

**Strengths:**
- Reasonable range: performance anxiety, phobia, emotional dysregulation, hidden core beliefs.
- Mariana's case teaches containment and timing.
- Fernanda's case introduces surface-level complaints masking deeper issues.

**Improvements needed:**
- No multi-session case demonstrating full arc of progress
- No case involving physical symptoms
- No failed or partially successful outcome case
- No grief/loss case
- No reluctant/skeptical client case
- Demographics are limited

**Recommendation:** Add 3-4 additional case studies: multi-session, physical symptoms, unsuccessful outcome, and skeptical client.

- Borrowing Benefits

- Matrix Reimprinting (at least mention)

- Working within a referral network
- Cultural considerations
- Group facilitation

**Research Literacy:**

## 8. Ethical Framework Gaps

**Missing:**
- Dual relationships guidance
- Scope of practice gray areas
- Transference and countertransference
- Practitioner self-care and personal work
- Confidentiality specifics (HIPAA or equivalent)
- Advertising and claims
- Power dynamics
- Complaints and accountability
- Sexual and romantic boundaries

**Recommendation:** Expand ethics from a subsection into at least a half-lesson, with scenarios for analysis. Provide a written code of ethics.

---

## 9. Specific Corrections

**Factual corrections needed:**

1. **Amygdala "12ms" claim** should cite LeDoux (1996) and qualify as "approximately."
2. **2019 cortisol study** appears to be Church, Yount & Brooks (2012), published in Journal of Nervous and Mental Disease. Verify date or correct.
3. **"TFT works ~80%" attributed to Callahan** — contextualize as clinical observation, not controlled trials.
4. **9-Gamut counting "1-5 then 5-1"** — Callahan's standard is "Count to 5" without reverse. Note as variation if intentional.

**Content additions:**
- Decision tree for technique selection
- Session recording and review protocol
- "What I Wish I'd Known" section
- Resource library (Craig's EFT Manual, Callahan's "Tapping the Healer Within," Feinstein/Eden/Craig's "The Promise of Energy Psychology," Church's research bibliography)

## Final Assessment

This course has a strong pedagogical structure and a clear, empathetic teaching voice. However, in its current form, it is closer to a "Level 1 introduction to EFT/TFT" than a standalone professional training. The most pressing concerns are the absence of gentle trauma techniques (safety-critical), lack of supervised practice, and insufficient safety and ethics infrastructure.

With Tier 1 additions → defensible foundational training.
With Tiers 1 + 2 → genuinely strong professional preparation course.
With all three tiers → among the better EFT/TFT trainings available in asynchronous format.
